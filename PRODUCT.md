# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary:
- Valorant players evaluating a companion app — want stats, store, party, social, wiki, replay without alt-tabbing or trusting ad-laden trackers.
- Privacy-conscious gamers who need proof the app doesn't harvest data or show ads.
- Team captains / duo partners coordinating comp and chat.

Secondary:
- Skin buyers checking store / Night Market quickly.
- Investors, partners, press assessing execution and trust signals.
- Developers who may contribute.

All land with a short attention span and need to know: what it is, why it's different, and what to do next.

## Product Purpose

VALSYNC is the privacy-first, no-ads tactical companion for Valorant players. It surfaces stats, store, party, social, wiki, and replay in one app — calm, fast, and quietly competent.

## Brand Personality

Tactical. Understated. Earnest.

The interface should feel like a tool a Radiant player would open mid-match: precise, private, and quietly competent. No hype, no gamer-neon theatrics, no corporate SaaS blandness.

## Anti-references

- Hype esports neon (BLITZ.gg / Porofessor.gg): screaming gradients, glow borders, ALL-CAPS streamer overlays.
- Generic SaaS cream/blue (Monday.com / Notion empty state): pastel hero, rounded corporate rhythm, forgettable.
- Over-glassmorphism mobile game UI: frosted glass stacks, radial gradients, juvenile decoration.
- Cluttered old-school stat trackers (OP.GG web): table-heavy, ad-crammed, hostile density.
- Ad-first "free" apps: banners, auto-play video, dark-pattern upsells.
- Delightful onboarding cruft (Duolingo-style): bouncing mascots, tooltip tours, confetti nags.

## Design Principles

1. Readout, not billboard — information first, decoration never competes.
2. Privacy is visible — the no-ads/no-tracking promise is surfaced immediately and repeatedly.
3. Tactical restraint — every color, line, and animation must earn its place in a mid-match context.
4. Player-native language — copy and structure feel made by people who play Valorant.
5. Trust before conversion — credibility signals (data handling, open stack, release readiness) sit beside the download CTA.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Ensure color-blind-safe information encoding; do not rely on hue alone for status or rarity indicators.
